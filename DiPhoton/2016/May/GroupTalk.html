Kaluza-Klein Theories

Theodore Kaluza and Oscar Klein developed these extra dimension theories in the 1920s, originally as a way to unify the electromagnetic and gravitational fields.

This was done by proposing that these separate fields were really components of a higher dimensional field.

Consider one periodic extra dimesion:

$$ x^5 \sim x^5 + 2 \pi R $$

The periodic dimension would have momentum,

$$ p^5 = \frac{n}{R}, n \in \mathbb{Z} $$

and, the equation of motion is:

$$ \partial_{\mu}\partial^{\mu} \phi^n (x^\mu) = \frac{n^2}{R^2} \phi^n (x^\mu) $$

This gives fields with mass:

$$ m^2 = \frac{n^2}{R^2} $$

At energies large compared to \(R^{-1}\) we should get KK states that can be found experimentally.

The current experimental constraint on extra dimensions of this type in terms of \(R\) is

$$ R \lesssim 10^{-21} cm $$

Really Small!!

An idea presented by Arkani-Hamed, Dimopoulos and Dvali suggests that this extra dimension is only accessible to gravity.

The size of the extra dimension is then only bounded by tests of gravity which has been measured down to about a millimeter.

$$ R \lesssim 1 mm $$

The Randall-Sundrum Model

Building on this idea, the RS model assumes the extra dimension takes the form of a circle with halves identified.

$$ S^1 / \mathbb{Z}_2 $$

This has two fixed points at \( y = 0\) and \( y = \pi R \equiv L \). On each of these fixed points is a 3+1 brane that enclose the 5D bulk.

Now that we know what our space-time looks like we need to figure out how this geometry changes what we measure. The first step in any calculations with a curved space-time is to find the metric.

We start with a 5D warped line element:

$$ ds^2 = e^{-2A(y)}\eta_{\mu\nu}dx^\mu dx^\nu + dy^2 $$

and the action:

$$ S = S_{H} + S_{M} = \int dx^4 \int_{-L}^{L} dy \sqrt{-g}(M^3R-\Lambda) $$

We are interested in the so called "Warp Factor" \(e^{-2A(y)}\). We must determine the function \(A(y)\).

To find \(A(y)\) we must use the 5D Einstein equations

$$ G_{MN} = R_{MN} - \frac{1}{2}g_{MN}R = \kappa^2T_{MN},\\ \kappa^2 \equiv \frac{1}{2M^3} $$

The stress-energy tensor in this case is

$$ T_{MN} = \frac{-2}{\sqrt{-g}} \frac{\delta S_{M}}{\delta g^{MN}} $$

Using the matter part of the Einstein-Hilbert action

$$ \kappa^2 T_{MN} = \frac{-\Lambda g_{MN}}{2M^3}, g_{55} = 1 $$

Then the extra dimension component of the Einstein Tensor

$$ G_{55} = 6A'^2 = \frac{-\Lambda}{2M^3} $$

\(A'^2\) is a constant we call \(k^2\)

$$ A'^2 = \frac{-\Lambda}{12M^3} \equiv k^2 $$

Integrating over y

$$ A(y) = \pm ky $$

We want this to obey the oribifold symmetry \(y \rightarrow -y\)

$$ A(y) = k|y| $$

And so, finally, we have the line element

$$ ds^2 = e^{-2k|y|} \eta_{\mu\nu}dx^\mu dx^\nu + dy^2 $$

where our warped metric is

$$ g_{\mu\nu} = e^{-2k|y|}\eta_{\mu\nu} $$

Now that we have our metric we can return to what we care about

Particles!

Consider the Higgs field with the action

$$ S_{Higgs} = \int d^4x \sqrt{g} [g^{\mu\nu} D_\mu H^{\dagger} D_\nu H - \lambda(H^{\dagger}H-v^2)^2] $$

We substitute the metric we found at the matter brane \( (y = L) \)

$$ S_{Higgs} = \int d^4x e^{-4kL} [e^{2kL}\eta^{\mu\nu} D_\mu H^{\dagger} D_\nu H - \lambda(H^{\dagger}H-v^2)^2] $$

Then to get a canonically normalized action we make the substitution \(H = e^{kL} \tilde{H} \)

$$ S_{Higgs} = \int d^4x [\eta^{\mu\nu} D_\mu \tilde{H}^{\dagger} D_\nu \tilde{H} - \lambda(\tilde{H}^{\dagger}\tilde{H}-(e^{-kL}v)^2)^2] $$

This is the normal Higgs action except the vacuum expectation value is exponentially supressed.

$$ v_{eff} = e^{-kL}v $$

Because the Higgs vev is responsible for the mass parameters in the SM, this naturally generates a heirarchy on the matter brane.

Considering that \(M_{W} \simeq 10^{-16}M_{Pl} \) then we can estimate the size of the extra dimesion

$$ kL \simeq 35 $$

More often in papers the quantity \( k/M_{Pl} \sim \mathcal{O}(0.1) \) is used.

\( e^+ e^- \rightarrow \mu^+ \mu^- \)

Summary

  • We have seen an outline of how the Randall-Sundrum Model is defines an extra space dimension.
  • We used the shape of this extra dimesion to calculate the "warp factor" and the metric.
  • This metric was then used to show how this model can generate a natural heirarchy on the 3+1 matter brane.
